## 1. General / About NIPM

### Q1. What is NIPM?

**A:** NIPM stands for **National Institute of Personnel Management**. It is the only all-India body of professional managers engaged in the profession of Human Resource Management (HRM), Industrial Relations (IR), Labour Welfare, and Training & Development. NIPM is a non-profit professional body devoted to building professional excellence in the field of HR.

🔗 **Learn more:** [https://www.nipm.in/about](https://www.nipm.in/about)

---

### Q2. When was NIPM founded?

**A:** NIPM was founded on **15 March 1980**. It came into existence as a result of the merger of two professional institutions:
- **Indian Institute of Personnel Management (IIPM)** — Established in 1948 in Kolkata
- **National Institute of Labour Management (NILM)** — Established in 1950 in Bombay (now Mumbai)

🔗 **Learn more:** [https://www.nipm.in/about](https://www.nipm.in/about)

---

### Q3. What is NIPM's vision?

**A:** NIPM's vision is:

> *"To be the most valued organisation of HR professionals in India and an acclaimed global contributor to HR Excellence."*

🔗 **Learn more:** [https://www.nipm.in/about/vision](https://www.nipm.in/about/vision)

### Q4. What is NIPM's mission?

...by leveraging own expertise, through collaborations, and by networking.

### Q11. What is the Code of Ethics of NIPM?

**A:** As a member of NIPM, every member pledges to follow **9 ethical principles**:

1. **Subscribe to Aims and Objects** — Be bound by NIPM's constitution
2. **Recognise Human Dignity** — Accept dignity irrespective of religion, language, caste, or creed
3. **Maintain Integrity** — Uphold high professional standards
4. **Act Responsibly** — Be committed to organisational goals
5. **Promote HR Practices** — Establish healthy Human Resource practices
6. **Foster Trust** — Win confidence of employers, employees, and stakeholders
7. **Uphold Professional Integrity** — Ensure confidentiality; no non-professional interference
8. **Avoid Improper Gratifications** — No improper rewards in professional work
9. **Safeguard the Profession** — Enhance the good name of the HR profession

🔗 **Learn more:** [https://www.nipm.in/about/code-of-ethics](https://www.nipm.in/about/code-of-ethics)

### Q13. What is NIPM 4000-2016?

**A:** **NIPM 4000-2016** is the Global Standards for Quality of People Processes. It is aimed at defining global benchmarks for HR processes in organizations. The NIPM 4000-2016 certification is the mark of excellence of people processes in organizations. It was developed by NIPM to help organizations achieve and demonstrate quality in their HR practices.

## 2. Membership – Types, Eligibility & Benefits

### Q16. What types of membership does NIPM offer?

**A:** NIPM offers **5 types of membership**:

| # | Type | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Associate Member** | Graduates beginning their HR journey |
| 2 | **Corporate/Annual Member** | PG holders with HR experience in active roles |
| 3 | **Life Member** | Eligible professionals seeking lifetime membership |
| 4 | **Student Member** | Students pursuing HR/Management courses |
| 5 | **Institutional Member** | Organizations, government bodies, educational institutions |

🔗 **Learn more:** [https://www.nipm.in/membership-and-eligiblity](https://www.nipm.in/membership-and-eligiblity)

---

### Q17. What is the eligibility for Associate Membership?

**A:** To become an **Associate Member**, you must:
- Have passed **at least a degree examination** or its equivalent

**Upgrade Path:** Associate Members can upgrade to Annual or Life Membership after acquiring any HR-related qualification and 3 or more years of experience in any field of HR, by paying the prescribed fee.

---

### Q18. What is the eligibility for Annual/Corporate Membership?

**A:** To become an **Annual/Corporate Member**, you must have:

- A **Post Graduate Degree or Post Graduate Diploma in Management**, the curriculum of which includes Personnel Management, Industrial Relations, Labour Welfare, etc.

**AND** one of the following:
- Holding any **managerial, administrative, or staff training position**, or acting as an advisor/consultant in any organization (Government, Semi-Government, public, or private sector) engaged in industry, trade, or commerce.

**OR**
- Working as an **academician for at least 3 years**, dealing with Personnel Management, General Management, HRM, Industrial Relations, Labour Welfare, or any related field.

---

### Q19. What is the eligibility for Life Membership?

**A:** The eligibility for **Life Membership** is the **same as Annual/Corporate Membership**. The difference is that Life Members make a **one-time payment** instead of paying annual renewal fees.

---

### Q20. What is the eligibility for Student Membership?

**A:** To become a **Student Member**, you must be:
- An individual **undergoing a recognised course**, either for a degree or diploma, in Human Resource Management, Industrial Relations, or Labour Welfare.

---

### Q21. What is the eligibility for Institutional Membership?

**A:** The following entities are eligible for **Institutional Membership**:
- Central or any State Government
- Any local self-government body or any of their departments
- Any statutory body or corporation (statutory or otherwise)
- Any organisation, institution, or association (whether incorporated or not)

The entity must subscribe to and be interested in the furtherance of the objects of NIPM.

**Institutional membership is categorized into 3 tiers:**
- **Category A** — 500 or more employees
- **Category B** — Below 499 employees
- **Category C** — Educational Institutions

---

### Q22. Can I upgrade from Associate to Annual or Life Membership?

**A:** **Yes!** Associate Members can upgrade their membership to either Annual or Life Membership after:
1. Acquiring any **qualification relating to Human Resources**
2. Having **3 or more years of experience** in any field of HR
3. Paying the **prescribed fee** for the new membership category

---

### Q23. What are the benefits of NIPM membership?

**A:** NIPM members are entitled to:

1. ✅ **Attend and participate** in meetings, discussions, lectures, film shows, and programmes organised by NIPM or its chapters
2. ✅ **Concessional rates** for Management Training and Development Programmes organised by NIPM, its Regions, and Chapters
3. ✅ **Free copy** of the half-yearly journal **"Personnel Today"**
4. ✅ **Free copies** of newsletters, bulletins, and other publications from the concerned Chapter
5. ✅ **Access to Library/Information Centre** at NIPM National Office and/or the concerned chapter

## Bonus: Additional FAQs

### Q85. What is the full form of IIPM?

**A:** **IIPM** stands for **Indian Institute of Personnel Management**. It was established in **1948 in Kolkata** and was one of the two predecessor bodies that merged to form NIPM in 1980.

---

### Q86. What is the full form of NILM?

**A:** **NILM** stands for **National Institute of Labour Management**. It was established in **1950 in Bombay (now Mumbai)** and merged with IIPM to form NIPM in 1980.

---

### Q87. What is APFHRM?

**A:** **APFHRM** stands for **Asia Pacific Federation of Human Resource Management**. NIPM has organized events in association with APFHRM, including NATCON 2025.

### Q88. What are the new Labour Codes?

**A:** The new Labour Codes refer to the **Indian government's consolidation of 29 existing labour laws into 4 codes**:
1. Code on Wages
2. Code on Industrial Relations
3. Code on Social Security
4. Code on Occupational Safety, Health and Working Conditions

NIPM regularly organizes workshops and seminars on implementing these new Labour Codes.
